    8 Things You Didn’t Know About Julianne Nicholson

    1. Started Her Career In 1997

    She’s been around for a long time since her acting career has lasted nearly two decades and is still going strong. It would be interesting to get down with anyone who started in the 80s or 90s and ask them how much the industry has evolved in the last 20 years. [1]

    This appears to be something beyond of her range, but it may not be because she appears reserved at times, but it has also been noted that she can ramp it up when needed and surprise people by bringing her acting skills to an entirely other intensity than many people have seen. [2]

    5. Drop Out Of School To Pursue Acting

    It appears that a few people have done this throughout cinema history, and it is not the worst thing anyone has ever done. Julliane is one of them, and happily, for her, it has worked.

    6. Met Her Soulmate On Set

    She married British actor Jonathan Cake in Italy in 2004; they met while filming an unaired HBO pilot called Marriage. They have two children, Ignatius Cake and Phoebe Margaret Cake.

    7. Outstanding Performance In Mare of Easttown

    In 2021, Nicholson appeared as Lori Ross, best friend of the titular Mare Sheehan, played by Kate Winslet in the HBO series Mare of Easttown. The series was a rating success for HBO, shattering viewership records. Her performance in the series earned her tremendous accolades, with critics praising her terrifying portrayal in the series finale in particular. Nicholson received her first Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Limited Series for the role.

    8. Guest Starred In Many Shows

    In 2011, she appeared as “Jess,” one of Dr. Lawson’s panic attack patients, on Royal Pains. That same year, she featured in Boardwalk Empire as a recurrent character of US Assistant Attorney General Esther Randolph.
