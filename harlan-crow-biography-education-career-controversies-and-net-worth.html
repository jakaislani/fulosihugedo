    Harlan Crow was born in 1949 in Dallas, Texas, is an American-Kittitian real estate developer. He is known for his association with the Trammell Crow Company, a real estate firm founded by his father, Trammell Crow, who was hailed as the “largest landlord in the United States” by Forbes magazine.

    Harlan Crow Profile

    Educational Background

    Harlan Crow attended high school at the Randolph-Macon Academy in Front Royal, Virginia. He later pursued his education at Emory University in Atlanta, Georgia, before transferring to the University of Texas, where he earned a Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) degree.

    Career

    Harlan Crow has had a diverse and influential career, primarily in the real estate industry and in various philanthropic and political activities. Here are key highlights of his career:

    Harlan Crow began his career as a leasing agent at Trammell Crow Houston Industrial, gaining early experience in the real estate sector. Harlan Crow managed the development operations of the Dallas Office Building division at Trammell Crow Company during this period.

    In 1988, Harlan Crow assumed responsibility for Crow Holdings, a company founded by his father. During his tenure, he diversified the company’s portfolio and focused on property management, contributing to the company’s growth and success. He currently serves as the chairman of Crow Holdings and was formerly its CEO.

    Harlan Crow is known for his significant contributions to Republican campaigns and conservative causes, with donations amounting to nearly $5 million. He co-founded the Club for Growth and has served on the board of the American Enterprise Institute. His political activities have garnered attention and controversy.

    In 2012, Crow and his family obtained citizenship in St. Kitts and Nevis through the citizenship-by-investment program, which raised questions about asset protection and financial transactions. His decades-long friendship with Supreme Court Justice Clarence Thomas has been a subject of scrutiny due to gifts, vacations, and property transactions involving Crow and Justice Thomas.

    Harlan Crow is known for his extensive collection of historical artifacts, including items related to communism and Nazism. His Dallas residence houses paintings by Adolf Hitler, statues of authoritarian leaders, and original paintings by renowned artists.

    Harlan Crow has an impressive private library containing 8,500 books and manuscripts, including historical documents from significant historical figures.

    Controversies

    Harlan Crow’s controversies include his significant political contributions to the Republican Party and conservative causes, particularly his donations to President George W. Bush’s nominees for the Supreme Court. His close and lengthy relationship with Supreme Court Justice Clarence Thomas, involving lavish gifts, vacations, and property transactions, has also sparked controversy and calls for investigations.
